History of Coffee

Coffee was first discovered in Ethiopia by a goat herder named Kaldi, who noticed his goats became energetic after eating coffee berries. By the 15th century, it spread to Yemen and became popular in Sufi religious practices and public coffee houses.

Image Source: Vecteezy

Rise of Coffee Plantation in Asia

In the 17th century, European colonizers introduced coffee to Asia, where countries like India, Indonesia, and Sri Lanka began cultivation. Today, Vietnam is one of the top producers, blending traditional tastes with modern coffee culture.

Coffee Replaces Tea in the New World

After the Boston Tea Party in 1773, drinking coffee became a patriotic act in America. Imports from the Caribbean and Latin America fueled its rise, making it the dominant drink in the United States.
